# Unit 7 Reflection

[Reflect on your journey here]
In Unit 7 I learned about the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and how to apply design thinking to solve real world problems.

First, I learned about the Global Goals. The 17 SDGs were created in 2015 by the United Nations. The SDGs were developed through a more inclusive process involving many countries and stakeholders than the MDGs were. The SDGs reconcile the three dimensions of sustainable development, economic growth, social inclusion and environmental protection. They are intended to be interconnected, so progress on one goal often leads to progress on others. Some of the goals are No Poverty, Quality Education, Gender Equality, Climate Action and Clean Water and Sanitation.

Then I learned how to use design thinking to advance a Global Goal. Design thinking is a user-centered process that follows these stages: empathize, define, ideate, prototype and test. For this unit I chose a Global Goal that I was interested in and thought about how I could raise awareness or solve a problem to do with it. I learned that websites can be powerful vehicles for social change. A good website can educate people, motivate action and connect people to resources.
