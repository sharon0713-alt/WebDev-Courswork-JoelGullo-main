# Unit 2 Reflection

In Unit 2, I learned about design, design thinking, and how telling a story can help make websites that are easy to use.

I learned that design is everywhere around us. Everything, from apps to posters to furniture, is planned out. Good design isn't just about how things look; it's also about making things easier for people. There is both art (colors, fonts, and layout) and science (user interface and user experience) in website design. UI is how a website looks and feels. UX is about how a person uses it.

Next, I learned about the Design Thinking Process. Design thinking means using design as a verb - taking action to solve problems. It is a user-centered approach that focuses on real user needs. The process includes steps like empathize (understanding the user), define (stating the problem), ideate (brainstorming solutions), prototype (creating models), and test (getting feedback).

I also learned about storytelling. Storytelling helps designers empathize with users by sharing their experiences and emotions. Telling a user's story makes it easier to understand their needs and design better solutions for them.

Overall, Unit 2 taught me that good web design starts with understanding people. Design thinking gives me a process to follow, and storytelling helps me connect with users on a deeper level. I now see design as a way to solve problems, not just make things look pretty.
